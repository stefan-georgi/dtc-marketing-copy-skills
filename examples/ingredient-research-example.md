## CoQ10 (Ubiquinol)

**Common dosage range:** 100-300mg/day | **Form in product:** Ubiquinol (reduced form), 200mg

### Key Studies
1. [Langsjoen & Langsjoen, 2014] — Molecular Aspects of Medicine
   Finding: Ubiquinol showed 3-6x higher plasma levels vs ubiquinone in bioavailability comparison
   Sample: n=12 | Duration: 4 weeks | Type: Crossover pharmacokinetic study

2. [Mortensen et al., 2014] — JACC Heart Failure (Q-SYMBIO trial)
   Finding: 300mg CoQ10/day reduced cardiovascular mortality by 43% and hospitalization by 42%
   Sample: n=420 | Duration: 2 years | Type: RCT, multicenter, double-blind

3. [Mizuno et al., 2008] — Nutrition
   Finding: 150mg ubiquinol daily reduced fatigue and improved physical performance by 34%
   Sample: n=17 | Duration: 8 weeks | Type: Double-blind crossover RCT

### Mechanism of Action
CoQ10 is an essential cofactor in the mitochondrial electron transport chain (Complex I and III), where it shuttles electrons to produce ATP — the cell's energy currency. Ubiquinol is the reduced, bioactive form that bypasses the conversion step required by ubiquinone. In plain terms: it's the spark plug your cells' power plants need, and the pre-activated form means your body can use it immediately.

### DR-Ready Claim Statements
- "CoQ10 levels decline up to 50% between ages 20 and 40" — Source: Kalen et al., Lipids, 1989 (Proof strength: Strong)
- "Ubiquinol delivers 3-6x higher blood levels than standard CoQ10" — Source: Langsjoen & Langsjoen, 2014 (Proof strength: Strong)
- "34% improvement in physical performance" — Source: Mizuno et al., 2008 (Proof strength: Moderate — small sample)

### Differentiators
- Ubiquinol vs ubiquinone is a meaningful differentiator — most budget CoQ10 uses the inferior oxidized form
- 200mg dose is at the therapeutic range (100-300mg); many competitors underdose at 50-100mg

### Compliance Notes
- Structure/function claims about mitochondrial support are permissible with proper disclaimer
- Avoid disease claims (cardiovascular mortality data is for internal mechanism support only, not consumer-facing claims)

## PQQ (Pyrroloquinoline Quinone)

**Common dosage range:** 10-20mg/day | **Form in product:** PQQ disodium salt, 20mg

### Key Studies
1. [Stites et al., 2006] — Journal of Nutrition
   Finding: PQQ deprivation impaired mitochondrial function; reintroduction restored mitochondrial content
   Sample: Animal model | Duration: 8 weeks | Type: Controlled dietary study

2. [Chowanadisai et al., 2010] — Journal of Biological Chemistry
   Finding: PQQ activates PGC-1alpha signaling, the master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis
   Sample: In-vitro + animal | Duration: N/A | Type: Mechanistic study

3. [Harris et al., 2013] — Journal of Nutritional Biochemistry
   Finding: 20mg PQQ daily improved cognitive function and reduced fatigue markers (CRP decreased 45%)
   Sample: n=71 | Duration: 8 weeks | Type: RCT, double-blind

### Mechanism of Action
PQQ activates PGC-1alpha, a transcription factor that signals cells to build new mitochondria (mitochondrial biogenesis). It also functions as a powerful antioxidant — cycling through 20,000 redox reactions vs vitamin C's 4. For the consumer: CoQ10 fuels existing mitochondria; PQQ tells your body to build more. Together, they don't just recharge old batteries — they install new ones.

### DR-Ready Claim Statements
- "PQQ is the only compound known to stimulate the growth of new mitochondria" — Source: Stites et al., 2006 + Chowanadisai et al., 2010 (Proof strength: Moderate — human biogenesis data is emerging)
- "45% reduction in CRP inflammation marker in 8 weeks" — Source: Harris et al., 2013 (Proof strength: Strong)

### Differentiators
- PQQ at 20mg is at clinical dose — most competitors use 10mg or omit entirely
- "Only known compound for mitochondrial biogenesis" is a powerful ownable claim

### Compliance Notes
- Mitochondrial biogenesis claim is structure/function — permissible with disclaimer
- "Only known compound" claim should cite the specific study; avoid absolute phrasing in ads

## Cross-Ingredient Analysis

**Synergies:** CoQ10 and PQQ work on complementary pathways — CoQ10 optimizes existing mitochondrial function (electron transport) while PQQ drives creation of new mitochondria (biogenesis via PGC-1alpha). This dual-pathway synergy is the foundation for a strong mechanism angle.

**Strongest proof cluster:** CoQ10 ubiquinol — multiple human RCTs with specific bioavailability advantages. The Q-SYMBIO trial (n=420) provides the hardest data point.

**Evidence gaps:** PQQ's mitochondrial biogenesis claim in humans is still emerging — most definitive studies are animal/in-vitro. The Harris 2013 human trial shows fatigue and inflammation benefits but doesn't directly measure new mitochondria in humans.

**Recommended mechanism angles:** "Mito-Recharge Complex" (dual-pathway), "The Ubiquinol Advantage" (absorption), "Cellular Power Plant Multiplier" (PQQ biogenesis)
