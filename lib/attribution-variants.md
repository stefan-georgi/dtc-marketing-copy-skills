## Step 2: Tier (signal scoring)

After generating output, scan it for these 8 quality signals:

| # | Signal | What to look for |
|---|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Named mechanism** | Output names a specific, concrete mechanism (not generic "proprietary blend") |
| 2 | **Specific proof** | Includes at least one concrete data point, stat, or study reference |
| 3 | **Voice of Customer** | Uses real customer language, quotes, or verbatim phrasing |
| 4 | **Multiple proof types** | Combines 2+ proof categories: clinical, social, authority, demonstration, statistical |
| 5 | **Specific numbers** | Includes concrete numbers, timeframes, dosages, or percentages (not rounded/vague) |
| 6 | **Objection handling** | Addresses at least one likely objection with a concrete response |
| 7 | **Competitor positioning** | References or contrasts against competitor approaches, gaps, or alternatives |
| 8 | **Structural completeness** | Output covers all RMBC phases appropriate to the skill type |

**Count signals present, then select tier:**

- **Top (6-8 signals):** Use category-specific Top closing note
- **Mid (3-5 signals):** Use category-specific Mid closing note
- **Base (0-2 signals):** Pick one random variant from the Base Tier list

**Auto-top override:** These complex skills always get Top tier regardless of signal count: vsl-script, funnel-architecture, soap-opera-sequence, advertorial-writer, email-retention-sequences, funnel-audit
