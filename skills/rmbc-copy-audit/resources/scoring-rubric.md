# RMBC Scoring Rubric

Scoring guide for the RMBC Copy Audit skill. Each dimension scored 0-25, total 0-100.

## R — Research (0-25)

| Score | Level | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 21-25 | Elite | ICP described with demographic + psychographic specificity; pain points use audience's own language; market awareness level explicitly calibrated; competitor landscape acknowledged |
| 16-20 | Strong | Clear ICP with specific pain points; language mostly mirrors audience; awareness level appropriate |
| 11-15 | Adequate | General audience identified; pain points present but generic; some audience language |
| 6-10 | Weak | Vague audience ("entrepreneurs"); pain points assumed not demonstrated; generic language |
| 0-5 | Missing | No evidence of audience research; copy could be for anyone |

**Key signals:** Named audience segments, specific dollar amounts or metrics, industry jargon used correctly, awareness-level-appropriate framing.

## M — Mechanism (0-25)

| Score | Level | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 21-25 | Elite | Named, novel mechanism that explains WHY the product works; specific and believable; clearly differentiates from competitors; connects cause to effect logically |
| 16-20 | Strong | Clear mechanism present; reasonably specific; differentiated from obvious alternatives |
| 11-15 | Adequate | Mechanism implied but not named or fully developed; partially explains how/why |
| 6-10 | Weak | Generic claim ("our proprietary formula"); no real explanation of why it works |
| 0-5 | Missing | No mechanism — just features/benefits with no explanatory framework |

**Key signals:** Named process/system/compound, specific explanation chain (A causes B which produces C), uniqueness claim with evidence, believability anchors.

## B — Brief (0-25)

| Score | Level | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 21-25 | Elite | Single clear big idea; angle perfectly matched to audience awareness; offer crystal clear with urgency; strong competitive positioning; emotional driver identified and leveraged throughout |
| 16-20 | Strong | Clear strategic angle; offer well-positioned; emotional driver present; mostly aligned to awareness level |
| 11-15 | Adequate | Angle identifiable but not sharp; offer present but could be clearer; some emotional resonance |
| 6-10 | Weak | Multiple competing angles; offer buried or unclear; awareness mismatch; emotional connection weak |
| 0-5 | Missing | No discernible strategy — copy is a list of features or random claims |

**Key signals:** One dominant theme throughout, offer terms explicit (what/cost/why-now), awareness-level framing consistent, emotional hook threaded through body.

## C — Copy (0-25)

| Score | Level | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 21-25 | Elite | Hook stops the scroll; proof is specific and stacked (testimonials + data + demos); CTA single, clear, urgent; mobile-optimized formatting; specificity throughout (numbers, names, outcomes) |
| 16-20 | Strong | Good hook; proof present; CTA clear; mostly scannable; generally specific |
| 11-15 | Adequate | Hook functional; some proof; CTA exists; some vague language; formatting acceptable |
| 6-10 | Weak | Generic opener; proof thin or absent; CTA unclear or competing; wall-of-text formatting; heavy on vague adjectives |
| 0-5 | Missing | No compelling lead; no proof; no clear CTA; unreadable on mobile |

**Key signals:** First 2 lines earn the next 2, proof stacking (3+ proof types), single CTA with urgency mechanism, short paragraphs, specific numbers over adjectives.

## Letter Grade Scale

| Grade | Score | Interpretation |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| A | 85-100 | Control-quality copy — ready to test |
| B | 70-84 | Strong draft — specific fixes will elevate to A |
| C | 55-69 | Functional but underperforming — needs focused revision |
| D | 40-54 | Significant gaps — major rework on 1-2 dimensions |
| F | 0-39 | Fundamental issues — likely needs full rewrite |

## Calibration Benchmarks

Use these to gut-check your scoring:

| Copy Type | Expected Range | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Strong DR control (Halbert, Schwartz style) | 85-95 | Deep research, named mechanisms, masterful execution |
| Strong CA Pro member win | 80-90 | RMBC-trained, specific, strategically sound |
| Competent freelancer ad | 65-75 | Decent execution, mechanism often generic |
| Generic e-commerce ad | 50-65 | Some research, weak mechanism, feature-heavy |
| Generic AI-generated ad | 30-40 | Formulaic, no real research or mechanism, vague |
| Corporate brand ad | 20-35 | No mechanism, no direct response structure, awareness mismatch |

If your score deviates significantly from these ranges for equivalent copy quality, recalibrate.
